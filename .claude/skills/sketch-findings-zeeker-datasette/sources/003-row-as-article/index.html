ACRA updates financial reporting standards to align with IFRS S1 & S2, effective 2027.

Singapore's financial reporting regime will incorporate climate-related disclosures for large corporates from the 2027 financial year onwards.

17 April 2026 · Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority · Source ↗

The Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) today announced updates to the financial reporting framework applicable to Singapore-incorporated companies. The changes, developed in consultation with the Accounting Standards Council (ASC) and the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), bring the domestic regime into alignment with the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards.

Listed companies and large non-listed entities with annual revenue exceeding S$1 billion will be required to disclose material climate-related risks and opportunities from the financial year commencing 1 January 2027. Smaller entities will have a phased runway through 2029.

The update places Singapore among the first jurisdictions in Asia to mandate IFRS S1/S2-aligned disclosures at scale.

ACRA Chief Executive Ms. Kuntha Chelvam noted that the transition will be supported by a tiered guidance package, including sector-specific worked examples and a dedicated small-business simplification pathway. Initial drafts of the implementation guidance will be released for public consultation by Q3 2026.

The announcement follows 14 months of stakeholder engagement, including workshops with the Big Four audit firms, the Singapore Chartered Accountants, and the Sustainability Reporting Advisory Committee. Feedback repeatedly emphasised the need for interoperability with both the EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive and the US SEC climate rules.
