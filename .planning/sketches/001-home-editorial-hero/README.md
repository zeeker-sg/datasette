# Sketch 001: Home editorial hero

## Design Question
The current home page reads as "white and plain, no design." What voice should the homepage carry — quiet and refined, editorial/broadsheet, or data-forward?

## How to View
```
open .planning/sketches/001-home-editorial-hero/index.html
```

## Variants

- **A · Refined minimal** — Evolves the current light direction. Bigger Fraunces display H1, stats column beside the hero, hairline rules above sections, quiet petrol accents on cards. Still calm; just crafted.
- **B · Editorial bands** — Dark nav, full-bleed hero on warm paper with 120px italic serif, numbered section labels ("№ 01"), a petrol stat band, data listed as editorial rows (not cards), dark CTA block. Civic broadsheet energy.
- **C · Data-forward vignettes** — The data is the hero. Live sync indicator, a real sample table previewed inline, vertical accent bars on stat strip, sparkline cards for "recently updated." Think The Pudding / Our World in Data.
- **D · Synthesis (★ winner)** — B's shell (dark nav, warm hero, petrol stat band, № section framing, dark CTA) with A's card grid for "Available data", accent border rotated through petrol/ochre/terracotta, mono index numbers + meta, large display row counts.

The sketch toolbar (bottom-right) lets you switch between three palettes: **paper + petrol** (default), **broadsheet** (cream + ink-red), and **petrol-ink** (dark mode).

## What to Look For
- **Typographic weight** — does the Fraunces display at 80–120px feel appropriate, or too loud?
- **Color rhythm** — do the accent bands in B give the "design" signal you're missing, or do they look themed/dated?
- **Content-to-whitespace ratio** — C packs more information per scroll; does that read as "designed" or "busy"?
- **The stat strip treatment** — centered big numbers on petrol (B), vs. vertical bars with small copy (C), vs. sidebar column (A)?
- **Cards vs. list rows** — the "Available data" section renders as cards (A), editorial rows (B), and sparkline cards (C). Which one wants 23+ items to scale to?
